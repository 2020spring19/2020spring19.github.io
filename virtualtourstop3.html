You have reached the third stop of your virtual tour! Read below to learn about the third of the five layers of the ocean, the bathypelagic zone.


The next layer is called the bathypelagic zone. It is sometimes referred to as the midnight zone or the dark zone. This zone extends from 3,281 feet down to 13,124 feet. Unlike the mesopelagic zone, the tempature of the bathypelagic zone is constant. The temperature never fluctuates far from a chilling 39° Fahrenheit. Here the only visible light is that produced by the creatures themselves. The water pressure at this depth is immense, reaching 5,850 pounds per square inch. In spite of the pressure, a surprisingly large number of creatures can be found here. Animals living in this layer zone rely on detritus for food or on eating other animals in this zone. The animals most commonly found are fish, mollusks, crustaceans, and jellyfish. Sperm whales will hunt at these depths on occasion to prey on giant squid.
